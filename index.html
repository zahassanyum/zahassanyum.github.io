About Me

What I am is a mystery. A fugacious formation of eternal stardust is what I see as myself. A complex bag of chemicals that serves abode to more infinitesimal life forms. I feel myself as an insignificant entity in a chaotic cosmos with a slippery consciousness. Conscious of the brief present, but alas, not of that which lingers of me as I evanesce and perish finally. My existence is that of a clueless soul among billions of senseless wanderers, with nothing to help emancipate me from this perfunctory situation. But hey, who’s complaining? Whatever works!

I am a programmer. I’ve been programming since I was twelve. I first began with the vintage C language and the primordial Visual Basic 5.0 (and 6.0). During that period, I developed a myriad of trifling applications, fortunately the relics of few of which can still be found somewhere on CNET Download.com. Soon thereafter, I took to web development and began my journey of learning HTML and a muddle of related thingamajigs, including the quirky JavaScript and PHP which, rumor is, stands for Particularly Hard to Program with. Since then, I’ve been freelancing online, building websites and stuff. At that time, in a world destitute of Fiverr or PeoplePerHour, forums.digitalpoint.com served a haven for freelancers and for a long time I picked up projects from there. Presently, I am working on my own stuff (or ‘startups’ if you could call it that), and learning the latest cool technologies (read toys) in the webosphere. My current objective is to learn Laravel, AngularJS and Python.

Aside from programming, I am a student of Computer Science, currently in my last year for a bachelor’s degree. Personality-wise, I’m an indecisive introvert who’s obsessed with perfectionism and K-Pop (here's my playlist on SoundCloud). I am deeply interested in the study of anthropology, social psychology and history of civilizations, and to a lesser extent, philosophy. I love watching movies, and I’m enamored of Asian cinema, especially Korean. I have so far watched a dozen of Korean dramas, a fact which consequently enticed me to learn the Korean language.
